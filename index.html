Invitation

Respected Sir/Madam,

With great honour and deep respect, we, the members of the All Santal/Adivasi Organisations of Gossaigaon Sub-Division under Kokrajhar District cordially invite you as our Chief Guest / Guest of Honour to participate in the celebration of the 171st National Integration Day of the Santhal Community (Ranajot HUL Maha) to be held on 30th June, 2026 at the Haraputa Sporting Club cum Library Football Field, Bhairiguri, under Gossaigaon Sub-Division, Kokrajhar District, Assam.

The Santhal Rebellion (HUL) of 1855 was a revolutionary movement led by Sidhu Murmu, Kanhu Murmu, Chand Murmu, Bhairo Murmu, Phulo Murmu and Jhano Murmu against the oppressive rule of the East India Company.

On 30th June 1855, more than 30,000 Santhals assembled at Bhognadih and launched one of the earliest mass uprisings against British rule.

The Government of Assam and the Bodoland Territorial Region have recognized the significance of this occasion by declaring it as a Restricted Holiday.

Your gracious presence will greatly inspire the community and honour the sacrifices of our ancestors.

Historical Background of HUL

The Santhal Rebellion, popularly known as "HUL", occupies a unique place in the history of India.

The movement was organized against exploitation, unjust taxation, land alienation and oppression carried out under the colonial administration.

The rebellion was led by Sidhu Murmu, Kanhu Murmu, Chand Murmu, Bhairo Murmu, Phulo Murmu and Jhano Murmu.

On 30th June 1855, thousands of Santhals assembled at Bhognadih in present-day Jharkhand and took a collective oath to resist colonial rule.

Their sacrifice, unity, courage and determination continue to inspire generations today.
